In Music Throughout the Years

Graphic Designers Who Shaped Music

Reid Miles

Famous For: Blue Note Records (1950s–60s jazz covers)

Style: Bold typography, minimalist layouts, stylish black-and-white photography.

"It’s all about rhythm — not just in music, but in design."

Storm Thorgerson (Hipgnosis)

Famous For: Pink Floyd (Dark Side of the Moon, Wish You Were Here), Led Zeppelin (Houses of the Holy)

Style: Surreal, dreamlike, and thought-provoking photo-based designs. Helped redefine what an album cover could be — almost like album covers as fine art.

"You don’t just design a cover; you create an atmosphere, a mystery."

Pete Saville

Famous For: Joy Division (Unknown Pleasures), New Order

Style: Minimalist, experimental, used scientific and abstract imagery with cool muted palettes.

"Design should be open to interpretation — like the music itself."

Barney Bubbles

Famous For: Elvis Costello, Ian Dury, Hawkwind

Style: Playful, rebellious, mixed retro graphics with punk attitude — sometimes coded his work with hidden messages.

"If you can read it easily, you’re doing it wrong."

Jean-Paul Goude

Famous For: Grace Jones album covers like Island Life

Style: Visually stunning photography combined with surreal graphic manipulation. Totally sculpted Grace Jones' visual persona.

"Photography isn’t just capturing reality — it’s inventing a new one."

Art Kane

Famous For: Music photography for The Who, Cream, Bob Dylan, Frank Zappa

Style: Bold color usage, narrative-driven photography that often felt cinematic and larger-than-life.

"A great photograph is a performance frozen in time."
